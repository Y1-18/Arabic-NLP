Arabic Poetry as Linguistic Evidence

The classical work "Abyat al-Istishhad" (Verses of Citation) is a foundational
text in Arabic linguistics. It compiles poetry verses that scholars used as
evidence for grammatical and lexical rules.

Key points:
- Poetry was a primary source for proving correct Arabic usage.
- Pre-Islamic and early Islamic poetry was considered the most authoritative.
- Linguists like Ahmad ibn Faris collected these verses systematically.
- The tradition continues to influence modern Arabic studies.
